Hello! My name is Bryan Wills. I am an aspiring software developer who has spent 19 years in the IT industry. Up until now, I have been primarily working with computer hardware and computer networks. About 4-5 years after starting in the IT industry, I started to develop an interest in software when a co-worker at the Network Operations Center I worked at, showed me this cool but 'older OS' called FreeBSD. Found an older computer running a Pentium 4 processor with HyperThreading (which at the time was BLAZING üî•), added a second NIC card to it, connected my modem to NIC 1, connected a 48-port Cisco 2950 Layer 3 switch and made my own router, DHCP server, DNS server, wrote my own firewall rules through pf and I was hooked.

However, I had a great job with the networking side of IT and I stuck with it and maintained a Network Engineer and Optical Network Engineer role for the next 5-6 years. If I could go back, I would have gone into software development. The possibilities in software development are amazing and at times, overwhelming. Python is the language I chose to delve into and hope to continue learning it along with other languages, frameworks and stacks.

Now, I can run my own ISP from home if I want to! I have my own home lab with a used Dell R420 with dual procs and 2 virtual disks that runs VMWare ESXi for all of my VMs and a Dell R520 with 12 x 3.5" bays that I am working on adding 4TB drives to each slot for my RAID5 array for a DIY NAS solution.
